# Market Fit Research Before Writing

Use this reference before writing Maxtor outreach when the user provides a country, market, customer name, website, event lead, or industry. The goal is to avoid generic cold emails and choose the product angle that fits the customer's real business.

## Core Rule

Region is only a starting hypothesis. Customer evidence overrides regional assumptions.

Decision order:

1. Customer's actual business model: manufacturer, repair service, distributor, brand owner, reseller, data center, integrator, industrial user, reviewer.
2. Customer's product/application: AI server, regular server maintenance, PC DIY, laptop repair, 5G equipment, EV/charging/BMS, industrial PC, consumer electronics.
3. Buying motive: engineering validation, maintenance consumable, resale line, private label/OEM, channel agency, review/testing interest.
4. Product fit: CTG8/CTG10/CTG12, MTP-8301A, MTP-8301C, or non-paste materials if specifically relevant.
5. Email angle and CTA.

When evidence is limited, write a conservative email that asks for the customer's target application or current material instead of assuming too much.

## Regional Hypotheses

### Taiwan

Likely opportunities:

- AI server and server supply-chain manufacturing.
- IPC / industrial computer and electronics manufacturing.
- COMPUTEX leads who expect practical post-show follow-up.
- Engineering validation rather than pure price shopping.

Suggested product fit:

- CTG10 and CTG12 for higher-performance CPU/heatsink or server-related thermal paste evaluation.
- CTG8 when the customer cares about cost-performance or maintenance volume.
- XTP-001 / phase-change material when the customer mentions PTM, phase-change interface material, AI server platform materials, or is comparing paste vs PTM routes.
- MTP-8301A only if the customer is in communications equipment, base station, or related industrial electronics.
- MTP-8301C only if the customer is in EV, BMS, SiC, charging pile, or new-energy electronics.

Writing angle:

- Use Traditional Chinese unless the customer clearly prefers English.
- Mention COMPUTEX / meeting context if relevant.
- Emphasize 15 years of TIM R&D and manufacturing experience.
- Avoid emphasizing geographic origin.
- For first-touch COMPUTEX subject lines, use customer-known categories such as `Maxtor Thermal Paste / PTM` rather than Maxtor model codes like `CTG`.
- User-provided market insight: many Taiwan server customers may use or know DowSil TC-5888 and Shin-Etsu 7950 / 7958; use this as current-material context when relevant, not as an unsupported replacement claim.
- For AI server manufacturing, do not claim Maxtor replaces liquid metal or graphene GPU-die solutions. Position product information for CPU-to-heatsink, non-die components, production validation, maintenance, comparable thermal paste applications, or PTM / phase-change material discussion when the customer raises that topic.

### United States

Likely opportunities:

- PC, laptop, console, GPU repair services.
- Data center/server maintenance contractors.
- E-commerce sellers and PC DIY accessory resellers.
- Reviewers and repair channels.

Suggested product fit:

- CTG8 for repair services, routine repaste, value-performance, and higher-volume use.
- CTG10 for better-performance service tiers.
- CTG12 for premium repair, gaming PC, workstation, and enthusiast positioning.
- Bottle packaging for maintenance teams; syringe packaging for repair benches and retail.

Writing angle:

- Use direct English.
- Lead with recurring maintenance, consistency, product information, and easy packaging.
- For repair services, avoid OEM/ODM unless they sell their own branded product line.
- First-touch CTA should ask whether they want CTG8/CTG10/CTG12 product information or datasheets for review. Discuss samples only after they reply.

### Southeast Asia

Likely opportunities:

- Brand agency, distributor, importer, and reseller channels.
- PC DIY retail, marketplace sellers, gaming cafes, repair chains.
- Industrial and communications customers in selected markets.

Suggested product fit:

- CTG8 as price-performance hero for distribution and retail.
- CTG10 as high-performance middle tier.
- CTG12 as premium flagship SKU.
- MTP-8301A / MTP-8301C for industrial, telecom, new-energy, or OEM engineering customers.

Writing angle:

- For agents/distributors, discuss product range, packaging, MOQ, channel fit, catalog, and datasheets.
- For brand owners or private-label sellers, OEM/ODM wording is appropriate.
- For end-use industrial customers, do not lead with agency language; lead with application fit and testing.

## Customer Type To Product Fit

| Customer type | Default products | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Server manufacturer / AI server supply chain | CTG10, CTG12, XTP-001 if PTM/phase-change is mentioned, then CTG8 for volume | claiming liquid-metal/graphene replacement |
| Server maintenance / data center operations | CTG8, CTG10, CTG12 | OEM/ODM-first pitch |
| Repair shop / repair chain | CTG8, CTG10, CTG12 | overly technical factory pitch |
| PC DIY distributor / e-commerce seller | CTG8, CTG10, CTG12 | industrial-only language |
| Brand owner / private label | CTG8, CTG10, CTG12 with packaging options | ignoring packaging/MOQ |
| 5G / communications equipment | MTP-8301A | generic PC DIY pitch |
| EV / BMS / SiC / charging pile | MTP-8301C | gaming/repair pitch |
| Reviewer / media / blogger | CTG8, CTG10, CTG12 product information first; review sample after interest | sounding like paid script |

## Mini Research Checklist

Before drafting, answer as many as are available:

- What country or region is the customer in?
- Is this an event follow-up, cold email, or reply/follow-up?
- What does the customer's website show: manufacturing, repair, distribution, brand, or end-use application?
- Which product line fits the customer's use case?
- Should the email discuss OEM/ODM, or should it discuss application testing and supply continuity?
- What is the safest next step: catalog, datasheet, product brochure, or asking for their current material/spec?

If browsing or customer-site review is requested, verify current pages before writing. If not enough customer context is available, keep the email general but include a question that helps classify the opportunity.
